My name is Alexis Antonio Arias, a professor at the School of Law, Languages and Common Cycle at the Universidad del Caribe. I also work as a technical teacher for the Ministry of Education of the Dominican Republic - MINERD. I am a lawyer with a Master's degree in Law and International Relations from the Universidad Autónoma de Santo Domingo.


In my approach to teaching, I prioritize the development of 21st-century skills through a variety of teaching styles. These skills include critical thinking, creativity, communication, and collaboration, which are essential for success in the modern world. By incorporating these skills into my lessons, I aim to equip my students with the tools they need to thrive in a rapidly changing society. I believe that by emphasizing the cultivation of these skills, I can help my students not only to acquire knowledge but also to develop the competencies necessary for lifelong learning and personal growth.


In addition to my work as a professor and technical teacher, I have also participated in various research projects related to the fields of law and international relations. I believe that research and scholarship are important components of effective teaching, as they allow me to stay up-to-date with the latest developments in my field and to bring new insights and perspectives into the classroom.


Moreover, I have had the opportunity to participate in several professional development programs focused on the integration of technology into teaching and learning. Through these programs, I have gained valuable skills and knowledge that have helped me to design more engaging and effective online courses.


Finally, I believe that teaching is a collaborative endeavor, and I have enjoyed working with colleagues both within and outside of my institution to share ideas and best practices. I am also committed to fostering a supportive and inclusive learning environment that values diversity and encourages students to express their views and opinions.
